Story

Here's the story of a little caterpillar that hatches from an egg. Right from the start from the day he was born, he is always hungry and starts eating different foods each day. However, in order to lessen his apetite, he ate lots of junk food. His overeating lead to feeling overly stuffed. A wise choice of a leaf the next day brings relief, and as time passes, the caterpillar grows up. The following days he built himself a cocoon and he emerged as a stunning butterfly!

Origin of the story

It is told that Eric Carle was inspired for this idea when he was punching some holes on some document papers. He came up with the following idea: a Bookwork character based on what he just did. A while later, he came with the idea to make it about a caterpillar. Immediately, he knew how he would design the book itself: the book had to have cut-out pages that all differed. He gained this idea from some books that he had read during his childhood, which he really enjoyed. The book was published by the World Publishing Company in 1969. Lesson behind the story

Although the book is very short, it has a lot of lessons for children in it. First of all, it introduces toddlers to all kinds of food and it teaches them what feeling very hungry is. When children get older, they can recognise the patterns in the book, namely that the amount of food that the caterpillar eats increases and increases and that a high amount of food is not good for you. Moreover, kids can also learn the names of days of the week by reading this book. Another lesson kids can learn from this book is the life cycle of a caterpillar and how it evolves into a beautiful butterfly

Effect on children's life

Once children understand the story of the Very Hungry Caterpillar, they will notice the eating pattern of the caterpillar. They will know that overeating and eating too much unhealthy food makes the caterpillar sick. Children can be more aware of the food choices they make in the future, because they now know that junk food is not good for you.
