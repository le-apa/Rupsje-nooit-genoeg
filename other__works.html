Other works by Eric Carle

Though Eric Carle has become famous for his masterpiece, the very hungry caterpillar, he has also written plenty of other books. Carle has stuck to his exclusively stuck to his specialty, the genre of colorful children’s books with little text and plenty of drawings. Having published well over 40 books, Eric Carle can certainly be considered a prolific writer. Check out some of his best works below

The Hole in the Dike

The historical story of a boy who stops a flood by putting his finger in a dike is classic Dutch tale about heroism. Eric Carle brought this story to an international audience by publishing an English version children’s book about it. He collaborates with Norma Green

Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See?

Carles first work was a book designed to teach children the names of animals. Published as early as 1967, two years before the very hungry caterpillar, this book was a strong start to Carles career that has withstood the test of time. The story is about a brown bear who runs into various differently coloured animals and names them.

1, 2, 3 to the zoo

Carles second work, written between Brown Bear, Brown Bear, What Do You See? and The Very Hungry Caterpillar, this books theme is teaching children to count. Each page of this book contains one more zoo animal to count

Does a Kangaroo Have a Mother, Too?

Published at the turn of the century, this book consists of a child kangaroo asking his mother if various different animals have mothers to. Of course the answer is always yes, and this repetition teaches children that animals also have mothers
